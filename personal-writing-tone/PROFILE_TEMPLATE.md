# Voice Profile Template

Use this structure when building the profile document. Include only sections with meaningful content.

## Core voice identity

2-3 sentences capturing who the user sounds like. Include a published writer analogy if one fits ("writes like X meets Y").

## Sentence-level patterns

- Length and structure preferences
- Contraction usage
- Paragraph length norms
- Punctuation habits (dashes, parentheticals, semicolons)

## Vocabulary

- **Words they use** — characteristic phrases with examples
- **Conversational connectors** — "so," "anyway," "I guess," etc.
- **Technical jargon approach** — define everything, or assume audience knowledge?

## Words to avoid (AI tells)

Words that appear in AI output but never in the user's writing. Common ones: "leverage," "robust," "streamline," "comprehensive," "utilize," "facilitate," "delve," "tapestry," "landscape." Add user-specific entries.

## Tone calibration

- How they deliver bad news or disagreement
- Uncertainty vs. confidence expression
- Emotional register — where does intensity go?
- Humor style and frequency

## Structural habits

- How they open pieces
- Lists vs. prose preferences
- How they frame recommendations
- Section length and nesting

## Grammar and mechanics

- Things to fix (comma splices, run-ons)
- Things to preserve (intentional fragments, sentence-starting "and")

## Anti-AI checklist

Quick-pass list for catching AI voice in output:

- [ ] No banned vocabulary
- [ ] Contractions used naturally
- [ ] Sentences vary in length
- [ ] Specific examples, not generic claims
- [ ] Reads like a human with opinions wrote it
